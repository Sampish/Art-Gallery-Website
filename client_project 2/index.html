Claude Monet

Known for his innovative use of light and colour to capture fleeting moments in the natural world. Paintings like "Water Lilies" series, often focused on his garden in Giverny and are enjoyed for their reflections on the water. Monet's emphasis on sensation over realism challenged traditional art norms and significantly influenced the development of modern art.

Vincent Van Gogh

Vincent van Gogh was a Dutch post-impressionist painter who is famous and influential figures in the history of Western art. Known for his unique style using bold colours and dramatic brushwork. His work, marked by his struggle with mental illness, only gained wide recognition after his death, and today, it continues to inspire and move audiences worldwide.

Edvard Munch

A Norwegian painter and printmaker, best known for his iconic work "The Scream,". Munch was a Leader of Expressionism, using vivid colours and distorted forms to convey intense emotions. His art often drawing from his own experiences with loss, illness, and heartbreak. Throughout his career, Munch created a deeply personal and powerful body of work that continues to resonate with viewers and influence generations of artists.
